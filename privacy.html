POLICY

Privacy.

No advertising cookies, no tracking pixels, and no sale of personal data. Our publishing integrations operate only on Sendlines-owned accounts.

This website.

sendlines.com is a static site. We do not create customer accounts, set advertising cookies, run tracking pixels, or collect payment information. Fonts are self-hosted. Product links go to our Etsy shop, where Etsy's privacy policy governs purchases.

Our YouTube integration.

Sendlines Shorts Publisher is an internal tool used only by Virtual City LLC, dba Sendlines, to publish approved videos to the Sendlines-owned YouTube channel. It is not offered to outside users and does not provide access to third-party channels.

The tool requests the minimum Google OAuth scopes needed to upload videos and verify the authorized channel: youtube.upload and youtube.readonly. It uses those permissions to verify the channel ID, upload an operator-selected video with its approved title and description, and read the resulting video status back from the YouTube Data API.

The tool does not access or retain subscriber lists, comments, private messages, payment information, advertising data, or YouTube Analytics. It does not like, subscribe, comment, message, or automate viewer engagement.

YouTube data storage and use.

Google OAuth credentials are stored in the operating system's encrypted credential store on owner-controlled hardware. They are never embedded in public code, written to the website, logged, sold, rented, or shared with advertisers or data brokers.

The official API client does not persist provider-returned video IDs, URLs, or raw processing status. Local publishing records retain only Sendlines-supplied approved metadata, a content-hash deduplication tombstone, the operator-selected visibility, and verification timestamps. A separate non-secret policy-acceptance receipt and bounded health evidence may retain readiness status, the current policy version, acceptance time, check time, channel-match result, dependency category, and an explicit marker that no credential value was stored. These records prevent duplicates, enforce posting limits, and prove that safety checks ran without storing credentials or provider identifiers.

Sendlines' use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including its Limited Use requirements. Google's own handling of information is described in the Google Privacy Policy.

Retention, revocation, and deletion.

OAuth tokens are retained only while the integration is authorized and needed for the stated internal publishing purpose. The public website is static and has no verified OAuth, upload, or token-revocation endpoint. It does not accept credentials or upload files. Those features remain fail-closed here rather than presenting a control that cannot perform the promised action.

The owner-operated client provides an explicit revocation command. Using it immediately sends the token to Google's programmatic revocation endpoint, deletes the local refresh token, and deletes all locally stored Authorized Data accessed or stored under that consent. Deletion occurs as soon as possible and no later than 7 calendar days. An authorized operator can also start this process by emailing virtualcityhq@gmail.com. The request must identify the authorized Sendlines account but must never include a token, secret, or password.

Authorization can also be revoked through Google Security Settings. An available, policy-gated health check can reconfirm authorization and the expected channel without storing provider identifiers. When that check receives a typed invalid_grant, it deletes the local refresh token and related Authorized Data immediately after detection. Other provider or network failures remain distinct and do not claim that revocation occurred. Authorized Data is deleted as soon as possible and no later than 30 calendar days after Google Security Settings revocation.

Ordinary business records created independently of Google authorization and containing only already-public video IDs, URLs, and publication timestamps may be retained for audit and duplicate-prevention purposes. Authorized Data is not reclassified as an ordinary business record and is not retained under this exception.

Our Pinterest integration.

When our Pinterest app is authorized, it accesses only our own business account: account information, boards, and pins, using the minimum scopes needed to publish our own product pins and avoid duplicates. It does not access other users' data, follower lists, messages, or payment information. It performs no automated engagement.

OAuth tokens are stored securely on our own hardware, never embedded in client code, never logged, and never shared. No Pinterest data is retained beyond transient processing during a posting run. Revoking the app's access in Pinterest settings invalidates the tokens.
