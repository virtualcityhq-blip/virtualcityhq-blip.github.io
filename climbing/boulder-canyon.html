THE PLACE

Rock before route.

Boulder Canyon stretches along Boulder Canyon Drive (Hwy 119) in Boulder County, Colorado, five miles west of downtown Boulder. The canyon walls are granite and pegmatite, hard crystalline rock formed deep in the crust and exposed over millions of years of erosion.

The backyard crag of Boulder's climbing community, home to dozens of walls and sub-areas from The Dome to Animal World to Castle Rock. Boulder Canyon spans a wide range of climbing styles and has shaped generations of Colorado climbers on its crisp granite edges and splitter cracks.

Field notes.

What is Boulder Canyon climbing?
Boulder Canyon is a major climbing area five miles west of Boulder, CO, with dozens of walls along Hwy 119. It hosts sport and traditional climbing on granite and pegmatite.
What is the elevation of Boulder Canyon?
Boulder Canyon runs from about 5,430 feet at its mouth in Boulder, Colorado up to about 8,228 feet at Nederland, per USGS topographic data for the Boulder and Nederland, CO 7.5-minute quadrangles.
What are the Sendlines Boulder Canyon prints?
Steel-blue engraving prints of eight real Boulder Canyon routes, each rendered in fine-line etching style with the actual route line traced from published documentation. See Etsy for current sizes and pricing.
Are the route lines accurate?
Yes. Every route line is based on published climbing documentation and community beta sources. This page intentionally avoids grade or pitch claims until those facts are separately verified.
Is AI used to make the prints?
The engraving style is AI-assisted: a human researcher documented each route from published sources and real beta; AI rendered the fine-line etching from that description. Every route line is traced from real documentation, not generated. Fully disclosed on every product page.
