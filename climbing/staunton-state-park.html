THE PLACE

Rock before route.

Staunton State Park sits in Park County, Colorado, about 45 miles southwest of Denver near Pine Junction. Its granite domes and walls were opened to climbing in recent years and now offer some of the most accessible backcountry granite climbing on the Colorado Front Range.

The park's granite domes, smooth, featured, and sun-drenched, bring a Yosemite-scale feel to the Front Range. Routes range from welcoming shorter cragging routes to longer dome adventures, all on clean granite in a quiet forested setting.

Field notes.

What is Staunton State Park climbing?
Staunton State Park (Park County, CO) opened to climbing in recent years and features granite domes and walls. It is known for bolted sport routes in a forested setting about 45 miles SW of Denver.
What is the elevation of Staunton State Park?
Staunton State Park ranges from about 8,100 feet to over 10,000 feet in elevation, per Colorado Parks & Wildlife and USGS topographic elevation data for the Pine, Colorado area.
What are the Sendlines Staunton State Park prints?
Steel-blue engraving prints of seven real Staunton State Park routes, each rendered in fine-line etching style with the actual route line traced from published documentation. See Etsy for current sizes and pricing.
Are the route lines accurate?
Yes. Every route line is based on published climbing documentation and community beta sources. This page intentionally avoids grade or pitch claims until those facts are separately verified.
Is AI used to make the prints?
The engraving style is AI-assisted: a human researcher documented each route from published sources and real beta; AI rendered the fine-line etching from that description. Every route line is traced from real documentation, not generated. Fully disclosed on every product page.
What paper and sizes are available?
