ABOUT

Real lines, from real places.

Sendlines is one person and a lot of elevation data. I design mountain and route portraits from the numbers, not from memory.

I start with one place and the line the data supports. USGS elevation data becomes the contour field. A documented route becomes the only saturated mark. No stock silhouettes. No invented ridgelines.

The climbing portraits begin with routes people actually climb, from Clear Creek Canyon to Boulder Canyon to Staunton. A route is a story you can trace. Some lines you clip. This one you hang.

BUILT CLOSE TO THE GROUND

The map is not decoration.

Sendlines is built by Jared Sacks in Colorado. The work starts with places he knows as terrain, not a generic mountain silhouette, and keeps the route, summit, and source data accountable all the way to the finished object.

The restraint is deliberate: enough context to recognize the mountain, one line to carry the memory, and no invented path where the source stops.

THE METHOD

How a line becomes a print.

I keep it small on purpose. I design each mountain and route portrait here in Colorado. Our U.S. production partner prints each order and ships it directly. If a peak or route means something to you, it holds a wall better than it holds a memory.

You looked at that wall. Now it looks back.
