THE PLACE

Rock before route.

Clear Creek Canyon runs along Highway 6 in Jefferson County, Colorado, about 30 miles west of Denver. The canyon walls are banded gneiss, 1.7-billion-year-old metamorphic rock streaked with pale quartz-feldspar and dark biotite, folded by deep geological time into wavy, swirling layers.

One of Colorado's most storied sport and trad climbing areas, developed over decades and stewarded in partnership with the Access Fund. Its walls rise directly above Clear Creek. Steep, featured, and richly textured with the distinctive banding of ancient gneiss.

Field notes.

What is Clear Creek Canyon climbing?
Clear Creek Canyon is a major Colorado sport and trad climbing area along Hwy 6, about 30 miles west of Denver. It is known for its banded gneiss walls and longstanding Front Range climbing history.
What is the elevation of Clear Creek Canyon?
The canyon floor near Golden, Colorado sits at roughly 5,695 feet elevation, per USGS Geographic Names Information System (GNIS) data for the Golden, CO 7.5-minute quadrangle.
What are the Sendlines Clear Creek Canyon prints?
Steel-blue engraving prints of six real Clear Creek Canyon routes, each rendered in fine-line etching style with the actual route line traced from published documentation. See Etsy for current sizes and pricing.
Are the route lines accurate?
Yes. Every route line is based on published climbing documentation and community beta sources. This page intentionally avoids grade or pitch claims until those facts are separately verified.
Is AI used to make the prints?
The engraving style is AI-assisted: a human researcher documented each route from published sources and real beta; AI rendered the fine-line etching from that description. Every route line is traced from real documentation, not generated. Fully disclosed on every product page.
