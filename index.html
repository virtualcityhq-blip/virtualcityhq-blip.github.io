FIG. 01–03 · PROCESS

How a line becomes a print.

The route comes first. We trace the mapped line, draw the surrounding terrain from USGS elevation data, then keep the final plate spare enough for the mountain to remain legible. The result is not an invented ridgeline or a generic silhouette. It is one place, one route, and the measured ground around it.
